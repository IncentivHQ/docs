The **TransferGate** feature lets you control how transactions are handled. You can whitelist trusted addresses, blacklist suspicious ones, set spending limits, or require multiple approvals with Multi-Sig to reduce fraud and mistakes.

## Whitelist & Blacklist Rules

<Steps>
  <Step title="Step 1: Open Rules Page">
    From the TransferGate dashboard, click **Rules** to access the Blacklist and Whitelist tabs.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Step 2: Add or Manage Blacklisted Addresses">
    In the Blacklist tab, click **Add Address**, enter the wallet address, assign a risk level, and add a reason.  
    You can later edit, remove, or view its history.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Step 3: Add or Manage Whitelisted Addresses">
    In the Whitelist tab, click **Add Address**, enter a trusted address, and save it. You can also:
    - Use **Quick Add** to whitelist directly from recent transactions.  
    - Add a nickname or reason for easier identification.  
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Pending Transactions Rules

<Steps>
  <Step title="Step 1: Open Pending Transactions">
    Go to **Pending Transactions** to see all transfers waiting for your decision.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Step 2: Review Transaction Details">
    Each entry shows:
    - Sender and recipient addresses  
    - Token type and amount  
    - Any risk flags  
    - Current status  
  </Step>

  <Step title="Step 3: Take Action">
    Choose how to handle the transaction:
    - **Approve** → Process it immediately  
    - **Reject** → Block and move to history  
    - **Cancel** → Stop it before execution  
    - **Batch Approve/Reject** → Handle multiple at once  
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Spending Limit Rules

<Steps>
  <Step title="Step 1: Go to Spending Limits">
    From the dashboard, open **TransferGate > Spending Limits** to set caps on transfers.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Step 2: Choose Limit Type">
    Pick the kind of limit you want:
    - **Per-Transaction Limit**  
    - **Daily or Monthly Limit**  
    - **Recipient-Specific Limit**  
    - **Token-Based Limit**  
  </Step>

  <Step title="Step 3: Set Thresholds">
    Enter the maximum allowed amount. Then choose what happens if the limit is exceeded:
    - Block the transaction  
    - Require approval or Multi-Sig  
  </Step>

  <Step title="Step 4: Save and Monitor">
    Your rules apply instantly. The dashboard shows widgets for usage, remaining quota, and blocked transactions, plus notifications when you near your limit.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Multi-Sig Rules

<Steps>
  <Step title="Step 1: Enable Multi-Sig">
    When creating or editing your wallet, select **Enable Multi-Sig**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Step 2: Configure Signers">
    Set up your wallet by:
    - Choosing the number of signers  
    - Setting the approval threshold  
    - Assigning trusted contacts as co-signers  
  </Step>

  <Step title="Step 3: Approve Transactions with Multi-Sig">
    Transactions require approval from the set number of signers, and progress is shown in real time.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Step 4: Use Social Recovery (if needed)">
    If you lose access, start recovery. Once your co-signers approve, your wallet access is restored.
  </Step>
</Steps>
